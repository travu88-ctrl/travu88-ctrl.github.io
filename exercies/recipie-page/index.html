Detroit Style Pizza

Thick, airy, and perfectly crisp on the bottom, this Detroit-style pizza has those signature cheesy, caramelized edges with a soft, fluffy interior. The garlic butter brushed crust, creamy marinara layer, and crispy pepperoni bake into the dough for bold flavor in every bite. Finished with cool stracciatella and a drizzle of hot honey, it’s sweet, savory, and just the right amount of indulgent.

Prep Time: 30 minutes (plus 3 hours proofing time)

Total Time: 3 to 4 hours

Cook Time: 15 minutes

Yields: 8 slices

Ingredients

The Dough

  • 2 1/4 cups bread flour
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon diastatic malt powder (optional)
  • 1 to 1 1/4 cups lukewarm water
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt

FOR THE PAN

  • 1 tablespoon neutral oil

GARLIC BUTTER

  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon chopped parsley
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 2 tablespoons parmesan
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Pinch of salt

SAUCE

  • 1/2 cup marinara sauce
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream

TOPPINGS

  • Jalapeño slices
  • Beef or turkey pepperoni
  • 4 ounces low-moisture mozzarella, cut into tiny cubes
  • 4 ounces Monterey Jack, cut into tiny cubes
  • Stracciatella (the creamy burrata filling)
  • Hot honey

Instructions

PREPARE THE DOUGH

  1. In a large bowl, combine bread flour, instant yeast, sugar, and diastatic malt powder if using.
  2. Add 1 cup lukewarm water and mix until the dough just comes together. Kneading is not necessary. The dough should be slightly sticky but not unmanageable. If it feels dry, add more water 1 tablespoon at a time. You can also use a stand mixer fitted with a hook attachment and mix just until combined.
  3. Cover and let the dough rest for 20 to 30 minutes.
  4. Sprinkle the salt over the dough. With slightly wet hands, pinch and fold the dough repeatedly until the salt is fully incorporated. Cover again and place in a warm spot. I like keeping it in the oven turned off with just the oven light on.
  5. Perform 3 to 4 sets of stretch and folds every 30 minutes. Lightly brush your hands with a little neutral oil to prevent sticking. Grab one side of the dough, gently stretch it upward without tearing, then fold it over itself. Rotate the bowl and repeat on all four sides. Each set should take about 30 seconds. Cover and return it to the warm spot between each set.
